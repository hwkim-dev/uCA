# Contributor License Agreement (placeholder)

The PCCX project intends to publish a Contributor License Agreement
(CLA) governing contributions to repositories that may be used in
commercial PCCX products. This page describes the intent and the
structure the CLA will follow. **The CLA itself is not yet
published.**

## Intent

- The contributor retains rights in their contribution. The CLA does
  not transfer ownership of the contribution.
- The contributor grants the project a licence broad enough to
  redistribute the contribution under the project's existing open
  licence and under commercial licence terms the project may use
  for the commercial track.
- The contributor warrants that the contribution is theirs to give
  (originality, employer-rights handling).

## Structure (intended)

- Apache-style permissive grant: the contributor licences the
  contribution to the project under terms broad enough to cover
  redistribution and sublicensing, while retaining their own rights
  to the contribution.
- Patent grant: the contributor grants a patent licence under any
  patent claims they own that read on the contribution.
- Defensive termination: standard reciprocal protections against
  patent litigation.
- Employer / agency clause: contributors who contribute under an
  employer or government agency state how that employer's or
  agency's rights are handled.
- Future-proofing: the CLA covers re-licensing under future PCCX
  open and commercial licence terms, so long as those terms remain
  consistent with the original grant.

## Why a CLA at all

PCCX's commercial track requires the project to be able to
redistribute contributed code under licence terms the original
contribution licence may not permit on its own. A CLA cleanly grants
that redistribution right without changing the contributor's
ownership. Without the CLA, every relicensing decision would
require contacting every contributor; with it, the project can
operate the commercial track without that lookup cost.

## Until the CLA is published

- Contributions accepted before the CLA is published are treated
  under the existing open licence only.
- When the CLA is published, the project will ask earlier
  contributors to confirm under it. Confirmation is voluntary; a
  contributor who declines keeps their contribution under the
  existing open licence only, and the project respects that.

## Authority

This page is a description of intent. The CLA itself, when
published, is the authoritative document. This page is **not** the
CLA, **not** legal advice, and **not** a substitute for reviewing
the CLA terms with the contributor's own counsel.
