# Commercial track

The commercial track holds the supported, contractual offerings that
sit on top of the open core. Customers on this track receive
deliverables and obligations that the open licence does not by
itself provide.

## Anticipated offerings

| Offering | Scope (intent) |
| --- | --- |
| **ProCore** | Hardened, integration-ready RTL bundles tuned for specific deployment profiles. Ships under a commercial licence with support obligations. |
| **Enterprise SDK** | Toolchain (compiler backend, runtime, integration scripts) maintained for paying customers, including private builds and pre-release access. |
| **ASICKit** | Tape-out-oriented deliverables: foundry-targeted RTL views, timing scripts, verification collateral suitable for an ASIC flow. |
| **Customer model porting** | Engineering work to bring a specific customer model onto a PCCX target (board or future ASIC), with deliverables defined in a per-engagement statement of work. |
| **Integration support** | Direct engineering support for board bring-up, driver integration, and validation. |
| **Certification** | A formal sign-off process attesting that a customer's integration meets PCCX compatibility expectations. |

## Pricing and terms

Pricing is **TBD**. No public price list is published, and any
pricing in informal communication does not bind the project. All
commercial-track engagements are governed by a written contract with
the project's commercial entity, executed under the applicable law.

## Boundary against the open track

A commercial-track customer also has full open-track rights to the
published open code under the existing open licence. The commercial
track does not restrict open-track use; it adds support and
deliverables that the open licence does not provide on its own.

## Boundary against the capital track

Commercial-track payment is a customer-vendor transaction: the
customer pays for deliverables, the project delivers under contract.
This is not an investment, not equity, not securities. Any
investment relationship lives under the [capital track](capital-track.md)
and requires its own separate written agreement.
