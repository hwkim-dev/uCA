# Contribution rules (draft)

The PCCX contributor policy is summarised here. The repository-local
`CONTRIBUTING.md` files in `pccxai/pccx`, `pccxai/pccx-v002`, and
`pccxai/pccx-FPGA-NPU-LLM-kv260` remain the authoritative
operational guide for branch naming, commit style, and PR shape.

## What contribution does and does not entitle a contributor to

Contributing code, documentation, or evidence to a PCCX repository
does **not** by itself entitle a contributor to:

- automatic equity in any PCCX-related entity;
- royalties, revenue share, or profit share on any PCCX product;
- employment, sponsorship payments, or consulting fees from any
  PCCX-related entity;
- investor returns, dividends, or rights to capital distributions;
- preferential pricing, free licences, or other commercial benefits
  beyond what the repository's open licence already provides.

Contribution is a contribution; it is not a securities transaction,
an employment relationship, or an investment relationship.

## CLA (pending)

Contributions to repositories that may be used in commercial PCCX
products require signing the PCCX Contributor License Agreement
(CLA). The CLA is intended to be Apache-style: the contributor
retains rights to their contribution, and the project receives a
licence broad enough to redistribute under both the open licence and
any future commercial licence terms.

The CLA is not yet finalised. Until it is published, this section
is a *draft* operational policy. Contributions that land before the
CLA exists will be re-confirmed when the CLA is published; if a
contributor declines to confirm, their contribution is treated under
the repository's existing open licence only.

## What contributors can expect

- Their contribution is reviewed against the technical bar described
  in the repository-local `CONTRIBUTING.md`.
- Their authorship is preserved in `git log`. The project does not
  rewrite history to remove contributor attribution.
- Their contribution is licensed under the repository's existing
  open licence at the time of merge. Any later licence change
  requires the CLA to be in place first.

## Who to contact

Until the CLA is published, contributors with concerns about any of
the above should ask in the project's GitHub Discussions or open an
issue on the relevant repository. Do not rely on private chat or
informal commitments from individual maintainers — the operational
policy lives in this file and the per-repo `CONTRIBUTING.md`.
