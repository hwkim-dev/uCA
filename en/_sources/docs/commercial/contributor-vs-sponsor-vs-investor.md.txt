# Contributor vs sponsor vs investor

The project keeps the four roles distinct because confusing them
creates legal and reputational risk for everyone involved.

## Separation matrix

| | Contributor | Sponsor | Strategic customer | External investor |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Pays the project | No | Yes (sponsorship payment) | Yes (commercial contract) | Yes (investment instrument) |
| Receives deliverables under contract | No | No | Yes (per commercial-track contract) | No (not by virtue of investing) |
| Receives commercial licence to closed offerings | No | No | Yes (per contract) | No (not by virtue of investing) |
| Receives equity / convertible / SAFE | No | No | No | Yes (per investment agreement) |
| Receives revenue share / royalties | No | No | No | No (unless explicitly contracted, separately) |
| Receives recognition (acknowledgement, listing) | Yes (`git log`, contributor list) | Yes (per sponsorship policy) | Optional, per contract | Optional, per investment agreement |
| Has voting rights or governance influence | No | No | No | Only as defined in the investment agreement and applicable corporate law |

## What each role is

- **Contributor** — a person or entity that submits code, docs, or
  evidence to a PCCX repository. Governed by the
  [contribution rules (draft)](../onboarding/contribution-rules.md)
  and the relevant repository-local `CONTRIBUTING.md`. Will be
  re-confirmed under the CLA when the CLA is published.
- **Sponsor** — a person or entity that gives money to the project
  with no expected financial return. Recognised under the project's
  sponsorship policy. Sponsorship is not investment.
- **Strategic customer** — a person or entity that buys a
  commercial-track deliverable under a written contract. Pays for
  delivery, not for a stake.
- **External investor** — a person or entity that holds an equity or
  equity-equivalent instrument in the project's commercial entity
  under a separate written investment agreement, executed under
  applicable corporate and securities law.

## What is forbidden by this separation

- Treating contribution as if it were a securities transaction.
- Treating sponsorship as if it conferred customer rights or
  investor rights.
- Treating a customer contract as if it conferred equity.
- Treating an investment as if it conferred deliverables outside
  the investment agreement.

## Mixed roles

A single person or entity may occupy more than one role over time,
but each role is governed by its own instrument. A contributor who
later becomes a sponsor signs the sponsorship instrument; a
contributor who later becomes a strategic customer signs the
commercial contract; a contributor who later becomes an investor
signs the investment agreement. The earlier role is not converted
or upgraded by the later one.
