# PCCX patent posture

Status: not legal advice. This document does not modify the patent
licence terms of the underlying repository licence (Apache License,
Version 2.0 — see [`LICENSE`](LICENSE)). It describes the project's
operational posture toward patents only.

## Public docs are not patent applications

Documentation, RTL, and reference flows in public PCCX repositories
are public disclosures and are not patent applications. Korea
operates a first-to-file patent rule; once an idea is publicly
disclosed by the project, the project's ability to obtain patent
protection on that specific idea may be reduced.

## Candidates are tracked privately

Patent candidates are not enumerated in public repositories. They
are tracked in a private docket under separate confidentiality
controls (see `docs/ip/private-disclosure-boundary.md`). The public
file [`docs/ip/patent-strategy.md`](docs/ip/patent-strategy.md)
describes the *categories* of candidates only, not specific
inventions.

## Intake process

A new candidate invention follows the intake described in
[`docs/ip/patent-candidate-intake.md`](docs/ip/patent-candidate-intake.md):

1. Identify the smallest novel claim and known prior art.
2. Classify as already-public, not-public-yet, patent-candidate,
   trade-secret-candidate, or defensive-publication-candidate.
3. If patent-candidate, file before any further public disclosure.
4. If trade-secret-candidate, treat under the trade-secret policy
   and keep out of public repos.

## Apache-2.0 patent grant

The Apache 2.0 licence already grants a patent licence from each
contributor to the project's users for the contribution submitted
under that licence. This document does not narrow that grant.
