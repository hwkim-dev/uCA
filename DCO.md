# Developer Certificate of Origin (draft)

Status: draft operational policy; not legal advice; subject to
qualified legal review before any binding use.

## Purpose

The Developer Certificate of Origin (DCO) is a lightweight,
contributor-friendly attestation that contributed work is the
contributor's own to give and is offered under the project's
licence. It does **not** transfer ownership.

## Sign-off (intended)

When the DCO is formally adopted, contributors will be asked to add
a `Signed-off-by: Name <email>` trailer to each commit, asserting
the DCO terms. The project will document the exact trailer wording
and the version of the DCO it points at when that adoption is
finalised.

## Retroactive policy

The project does **not** retroactively require sign-off for past
contributions. The DCO, when adopted, applies to commits made after
the formal adoption date. Past contributions remain governed by the
licence in effect at the time of merge.

## CLA placeholder

A separate Contributor Licence Agreement (CLA) is intended for
contributions that may be redistributed under future commercial
PCCX licence terms beyond the open licence; see
[`docs/ip/contributor-license-agreement.md`](docs/ip/contributor-license-agreement.md).
The CLA is not yet finalised and is not required at this time.

## Future PR template

When the DCO and CLA are formally adopted, the project will publish:

- a PR template with a sign-off checkbox;
- a CI check that verifies trailer presence;
- a one-paragraph summary in `CONTRIBUTING.md` linking to this page.

Until then, this file is informational only.
